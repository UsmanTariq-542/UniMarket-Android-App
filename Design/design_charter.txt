# University Marketplace App  
## Project Charter (One-Page Formal Report)

**Project Title:** University Marketplace App (Semester Project Idea)  
**Project Type:** Mobile Application Prototype  
**Date:** March 26, 2026  
**Prepared For:** Internal Design/Planning Use (Design Folder)

### 1. Project Background
University students frequently need a safe and convenient way to buy and sell used items (books, gadgets, accessories, etc.) within campus. Public marketplaces often create trust and safety concerns. This project proposes a campus-focused mobile marketplace that connects students in a controlled environment.

### 2. Project Objective
The objective is to develop a mobile marketplace application for university students to trade products within their campus community. The app will provide:
- Secure student-focused access
- Simple product listing and discovery
- Communication between buyers and sellers
- A structured and trusted alternative to open public platforms

### 3. Scope Definition
**In Scope**
- Mobile app development for university students
- User registration and authentication (individual accounts)
- Product listing with category and image support
- Search and filter functionality
- In-app buyer-seller messaging
- Basic profile management
- Core UI screens:
  - Splash
  - Login/Signup
  - Home
  - Categories
  - Product Detail
  - Add Product
  - Chat
  - Profile

**Out of Scope**
- External payment gateway integration
- Nationwide/open public marketplace deployment
- Delivery/logistics management
- Multi-university rollout (initially limited to PUCIT department)

### 4. Stakeholders
**Primary Stakeholders**
- University Students (buyers/sellers)
- Project Supervisor (academic guidance and evaluation)
- Development Team (design, development, testing, delivery)

**Secondary Stakeholders**
- University Administration (potential future adopter)
- Future Sponsors/Investors (for scale beyond prototype)

### 5. Key Milestones
1. Project approval and charter finalization  
2. Requirements gathering and feature finalization  
3. UI/UX design and Figma prototype completion  
4. Database and backend design  
5. Frontend mobile development  
6. Integration and system testing  
7. Bug fixing and refinement  
8. Final submission and demonstration

### 6. Assumptions and Constraints
**Assumptions**
- Students will use university email for authentication
- Target users have compatible smartphones
- Required development tools/resources are available
- Campus environment supports peer-to-peer trading behavior

**Constraints**
- Limited semester timeline
- Limited budget and team size
- Prototype-level implementation target
- Initial deployment restricted to one university department (PUCIT)

### 7. Expected Outcome
A functional prototype demonstrating a trusted campus marketplace where students can register, post listings, discover items, and chat securely within the university context. The output will be suitable for academic evaluation and future enhancement planning.

### 8. Success Criteria
- Core in-scope features are implemented and demonstrable
- User flow (signup → list item → browse/search → contact seller) works end-to-end
- Prototype is stable enough for final presentation
- Out-of-scope boundaries are respected to maintain feasibility
